PICYCLE

Why picycle?

Do you have an unused Raspberry Pi board locked away in a cupboard? Please donate it to be used in scientific research ...

Our academic research project is investigating the use of single-board computers (like Rasberry Pi devices) for micro-datacenters and federated micro-clusters. Currently, we have around 1000 Raspberry Pi boards at the various UK university sites (Cambridge, Glasgow, Loughborough and Southampton). We want to scale this number up, to build a distributed UK-wide testbed for system experiments.

How to picycle?

(1) Put your Raspberry Pi back into its original box. If you do not have a box, wrap it in an antistatic bag or a sheet of paper.

(2) Put your packaged pi into a Jiffy bag.

(3) Address the parcel FREEPOST PICYCLE. No stamp needed!

(Math.PI) OPTIONAL: include a note with

  • your email address, if you want acknowledgement when we receive your donation.
  • your full name, if you want to be listed on our Wall of Fame

(4) Drop your parcel in a Royal Mail post box.

Objectives

We aim to break the Guinness World Record for the Largest Raspberry Pi Cluster. We believe this record is currently held by the Seemore 256-node cluster.

We also want to break into one of the energy-efficient supercomputing league tables, perhaps Green500 or GreenGraph500.

Finally, we see Raspberry Pi based federated clusters as a means of tackling computational inequality across the world. We support social enterprises with Networks4Learning and Madanyu, deploying Raspberry Pi solutions in Africa. We are using Raspberry Pi to support social networking in rural Amazon communities. Further, we want to use our cluster devices to support the #dataforgood initiatives, providing light-weight, low-power compute devices for big data processing in life-saving scenarios.
